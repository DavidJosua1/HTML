DNS, or the Domain Name System, acts as the internet's phonebook, translating human-readable domain names (like www.google.com) into machine-readable IP addresses (like 172.217.160.142) that computers use to communicate. When you type a website into your browser, DNS servers look up the corresponding IP address, allowing your device to find and connect to the correct server.
